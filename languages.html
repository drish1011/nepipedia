Major Languages of Nepal

Nepal is a multilingual country with a rich tapestry of languages reflecting its diverse culture and ethnic groups. Here are the ten most widely spoken languages in Nepal:

Nepali

Nepali is the official language of Nepal and is spoken by about 44% of the population. It serves as the lingua franca among various ethnic groups.

Maithili

Maithili is spoken by around 12% of the population, primarily in the eastern Terai region. It is an important cultural language with rich literary traditions.

Tharu

Tharu is spoken by approximately 6% of the population, mainly by the Tharu ethnic group in the Terai. It has several dialects and is closely tied to their cultural identity.

Tamang

Tamang is spoken by around 5% of the population, primarily in the central hilly regions. It is associated with the Tamang community and has a rich oral tradition.

Urdu

Urdu is spoken by about 5% of the population, particularly among the Muslim communities. It serves as a bridge language for many communities in Nepal.

English

English is widely used in education, business, and government. It is the medium of instruction in many schools and is spoken by a significant number of people.

Rajbanshi

Rajbanshi is spoken by around 3% of the population, mainly in the eastern part of Nepal. It is significant for its cultural expressions and heritage.

Bhojpuri

Bhojpuri is spoken by approximately 3% of the population, primarily in the southern Terai region. It has a vibrant cultural scene with music and literature.

Newari

Newari is spoken by the Newar community in the Kathmandu Valley. It is an important language for cultural identity and heritage, with a rich history.
