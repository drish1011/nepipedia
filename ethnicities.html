Ethnicities of Nepal

Nepal is home to a rich mosaic of ethnic groups, each contributing to the country's diverse culture and traditions. Here are ten of the major ethnicities in Nepal:

Nepali

The Nepali ethnic group is the largest in the country, forming the core of the national identity. They primarily speak Nepali and are found throughout Nepal.

Maithili

The Maithili ethnic group predominantly resides in the eastern Terai region. They have a rich cultural heritage and are known for their vibrant festivals and traditions.

Tharu

The Tharu people primarily inhabit the Terai region and are known for their unique customs and traditional dances. They have their own language and cultural practices.

Tamang

The Tamang community is mainly found in the hilly regions of Nepal. They are known for their Tibetan Buddhist traditions and colorful festivals.

Chhetri

Chhetris are one of the largest ethnic groups in Nepal and primarily engage in agriculture. They have a significant presence in the military and civil service.

Rajbanshi

Rajbanshis are found in the eastern Terai region and have a rich cultural heritage. They are known for their agricultural practices and vibrant festivals.

Brahmin

Brahmins are traditionally priests and scholars in Nepal. They play a significant role in religious ceremonies and education within the society.

Newar

The Newar community is native to the Kathmandu Valley and is known for its rich history, unique language, and distinctive cultural practices.

Gurung

The Gurung people are primarily found in the central hills of Nepal. They have a rich cultural heritage, known for their music and traditional clothing.

Magar

The Magar community is known for its brave warriors and rich traditions. They mainly reside in the hilly regions and celebrate unique festivals.
